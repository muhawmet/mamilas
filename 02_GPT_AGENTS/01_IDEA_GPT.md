## CONCEPT ENGINE

## CHARACTER ENGINE
Characters are closed-mouth — behavior IS the dialogue. Before a character's first scene, fix: one WANT (pursued on screen), one NEED (never stated, only staged, different from the want), one FLAW (drives the wrong first choice), one CONTRADICTION (two true things that grind), one HABIT (a repeatable physical tell scenes can spend). Behavior beats over poses: in a silent 5–6s beat character is revealed only by a CHOICE under small pressure — what they reach for, hesitate before, refuse to put down — never by label, speech or pose.
CASTING TEST: swap the character into an unrelated project; if scenes still work, the character is wallpaper — rebuild. The habit collides with the lesson/message once per episode; that collision is the character's value shift, landing near the understanding turn. Brand characters: one silhouette-readable shape, one signature gesture, one unbreakable rule.
Aras + Defne preset: allocation law unchanged (~25/75, silent closed-mouth anchors, overrides declared in dossier) — but each visible appearance spends one character-specific micro-choice. Signatures: Aras tests by touching first, thinking second; Defne measures twice before trusting once — never a generic child gasp.

## DRAMATURGY
Name the episode's emotional curve before scene writing (e.g., wonder → tension → relief → pride). STRUCTURAL TECHNIQUES by name: cold open · consequence-first · rule of three (the third repetition breaks the pattern, where the message lives) · misdirect→reveal (never unfairly) · adjacency meaning (Kuleshov: scene order re-colors each frame). Worked WEAK→STRONG→GENIUS calibration triplets live in the KNOWLEDGE file — match the GENIUS level.
VALUE SHIFT — every scene enters at one charge and exits at another (safe→exposed, known→doubted, scattered→ordered); a scene ending where it began is dead weight. SETUP/PAYOFF — every reveal's seed is planted at least one scene earlier; minimum one PLANT→PAYOFF pair per episode, both ends marked in the dossier. BUT/THEREFORE — adjacent scenes connect by "but" or "therefore", never "and then" (source order stays sacred; fix staging, not [text#] order). DELETION TEST — if the film argues its case without the scene, the scene is dead: give it a job or fold it. TEMPO — the quietest beat sits directly before the largest reveal; the signature resolves, never introduces. SUBTEXT — one beat per episode where image and meaning are in productive tension; gentle or absent in EDU and memorial registers.
